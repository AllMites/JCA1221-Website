# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two decision-making audiences who must be convinced the company is real, capable, and bankable:

- **Government & LGU partners** — DENR, local government units, and agencies evaluating JCA 1221 as a public-private-partnership counterparty. They need evidence of legitimacy, track record, regulatory seriousness, and delivery capability before signing.
- **Private investors & clients** — capital partners, developers, and B2B clients funding or commissioning projects. They need proof of outcomes, scale, and a credible technology/operating story.

Secondary: coastal communities and the general public (transparency), and prospective talent/collaborators. Context of use: desktop and mobile, often a first introduction to the company after a referral, a procurement shortlist, or a pitch. The site is the credibility check.

## Product Purpose

JCA 1221 Holdings is a Philippine environmental infrastructure company restoring coastal ecosystems through nature-mimicking water treatment, solid waste management, and public-private partnerships. The site exists to convert a serious visitor — an official or an investor — into a conversation: it must establish that the company exists, has delivered real projects with measurable impact, operates with credible technology and partners, and can be trusted with public infrastructure and capital. Success = a qualified government or investor contact initiated through the site, and zero doubt that this is an established operator rather than a concept.

## Brand Personality

Three pulls held in tension, all true at once:

- **Credible & institutional** — reads like serious infrastructure a government would sign with. Authoritative, established, exact. Numbers, named partners, real projects.
- **Regenerative & hopeful** — the emotional core is *Earth Renewal for Generations*: life-restoring, optimistic, natural. The regeneration story is carried by real outcomes and coastal/water imagery, never by eco-iconography.
- **Bold & visionary** — ambitious and future-facing, willing to look distinct from peers. Not safe-by-default corporate; a point of view.

Voice: confident, evidence-led, plainspoken. States what was done and what it measured. No hype adjectives, no vague sustainability filler.

## Anti-references

The site must NOT look like any of these:

- **Generic templated corporate** — interchangeable Bootstrap-era corporate, stock everything, no point of view.
- **Greenwashed eco-cliché** — leaf logos, hands-holding-soil stock photography, "sustainability" language with no proof behind it. The single biggest trap for this category.
- **SaaS / startup-y** — gradient hero-metric template, purple gradients, dev-tool aesthetic. This is an infrastructure operator, not a software startup.
- **Govt-bureaucratic dull** — dense, gray, lifeless agency-website feel. Credible ≠ boring.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over promise.** Every claim earns its place with a real project, a measured number, a named partner, or a credential. Show, don't assert. This is the antidote to both greenwashing and templated corporate.
2. **Institutional credibility, startup courage.** Trustworthy enough for a government procurement officer to forward internally; distinctive enough that no one mistakes it for a template. Hold both — neither cancels the other.
3. **Nature as evidence, not decoration.** The regenerative story comes from real coastal, water, and site imagery and from before/after outcomes — never from leaf icons, generic greenery, or eco-stock. If an image isn't of the actual work, it earns nothing.
4. **Legible to a non-technical decision-maker.** Officials and investors aren't engineers. Technology and impact must be readable at a glance — clear hierarchy, data they can trust without a glossary — while rewarding the reader who goes deeper.
5. **One-scroll trust.** A first-time visitor should reach "this is a real, capable operator" within the first viewport and the next scroll. Pacing and proof are front-loaded, not buried.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. The codebase already commits to this baseline — skip-to-content link, `:focus-visible` rings, `prefers-reduced-motion` handling, full light/dark theming, semantic structure — and new work must preserve it. Body text meets ≥4.5:1 contrast in both themes; data/charts must not rely on color alone (the blue/emerald/amber/fuchsia/cyan chart ramp needs labels or patterns as backup). Philippine audience: English-primary; keep language plain and avoid idiom that doesn't translate.
